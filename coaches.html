RPCA — 03 · Coaches

The Coaches.

Coaching from people who've actually stood in the middle — technique, temperament and honest correction, not drills copied off a screen.

Taught by players

No borrowed drills off the internet. Just coaching from people who've stood in the middle — and know what actually holds up out there.

Philosophy

Technique-first. Get the fundamentals right and everything downstream — runs, wickets, temperament — gets easier.

Ratios

Small batches so every player is seen, corrected and pushed each session — never lost in a queue.

Correction

Honest, every session. You'll always know what you did well and the one thing to fix next.

Edge

Current & competitive — coaches who are still in the game keep the coaching sharp and real.

AMCoach
Coach

Ashutosh Mishra

One half of the RPCA coaching partnership — Ashutosh and Yuvraj run the academy side by side, two young coaches of the same generation. His belief is simple and stubborn: build the cricketer from the ground up. Technique-first and unhurried — no shortcuts, no chasing quick results before the basics can carry the weight.

Under him, the fundamentals are drilled until they hold under pressure — the grip, the base, the shape of a shot repeated until it doesn't fall apart when the game gets hard. It's slow work, and it's the work that lasts.

  • 01

    Technique First

    Clean, repeatable fundamentals before anyone worries about a scoreboard.

  • 02

    Long-Term Development

    Players built over years — the next stage always in view, never rushed.

  • 03

    Standards & Discipline

    Punctuality, effort and respect — the habits that make cricketers before runs do.

Coach · All-Rounder
Coach · All-Rounder

Yuvraj Kumar

Yuvraj is a young Jharkhand all-rounder registered with the JSCA and an active Jharkhand T20 player for the Ranchi Titans — a coach who is still very much in the middle of competitive cricket himself.

That currency shows in his coaching: batting, bowling and match temperament taught by someone who solves the same problems on the weekend. In Jharkhand T20 2026 he was a Match Hero, scoring 38 off 23 balls.

  • 01

    Batting & Match Temperament

    Building an innings and reading the moment — the calm to bat when it matters.

  • 02

    Bowling

    All-rounder's craft — control, variations and the discipline to bowl to a plan.

  • 03

    Current Competitive Edge

    Still playing at state T20 level — the coaching stays sharp and real.

How we coach

A method, not a mood.

The coaching doesn't change with who's watching or how the day's going. Same standards, same honesty, every session.

  • 01

    Small Batches, Tight Ratios

    Few enough players that every one is seen and corrected — not managed in a crowd.

  • 02

    Honest Correction Every Session

    Real feedback, kindly given — you always leave knowing what to work on next.

  • 03

    Technique Before Results

    Get the method right and the scoreboard follows; chase the scoreboard first and neither lasts.

  • 04

    A Clear Next Step

    Every player leaves with one specific thing to build on — progress you can actually see.
