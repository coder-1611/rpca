01 — The Pathway

One delivery,
three stages.

Every player at RPCA follows a structured arc — from the fundamentals of grip and balance to a real, measured route toward district and state cricket.

01Ages 7–12

Foundation

Grip, stance, balance and a love for the game. Small groups, soft-ball to hard-ball progression, and the habits — punctuality, effort, respect — that make cricketers before runs do.

Junior batches →
02Ages 13–16

Development

Technique under pressure. Bowling machine, video analysis and match-simulation nets. This is where a decent player becomes a dependable one — read length, build an innings, hold a spell.

Youth squads →
03Ages 17+

Elite Pathway

The state route. Strength & conditioning, competitive fixtures and preparation for JSCA trials and league cricket. RPCA players have gone on to represent Ranchi sides in Jharkhand T20.

Performance program →
02 — The Ground

A standard,
not just a net.

DIG Ground, Bariatu · Ranchi

Nothing fancy — two turf & matting nets at DIG Ground in Bariatu, a bowling machine, and a coach watching every ball. What matters is the coaching, and that a school kid or a working student can still get a proper session in.

  • 01

    Turf & Matting Nets

    Two nets at DIG Ground, Bariatu — true bounce and enough volume to actually groove a change.

  • 02

    Bowling Machine

    Programmable pace and length to groove a shot or drill a weakness, over after over.

  • 03

    Video Analysis

    Slow-motion breakdown of technique — see the flaw, then fix it in the same session.

  • 04

    Strength & Conditioning

    A conditioning setup that builds the engine competitive cricket actually demands.
